How to Optimize Sitemaps for E-commerce

Published on July 21, 2025

E-commerce websites often have thousands of product pages, categories, and dynamic content, making XML sitemaps crucial for SEO. Here’s how to optimize your sitemap for an e-commerce site:

  • Prioritize Key Pages: Set higher priority (0.8-1.0) for homepage, category pages, and top products. Use lower priority (0.4-0.6) for less critical pages.
  • Update Frequently: Set to daily for product and category pages with frequent updates.
  • Include Last Modified Dates: Ensure reflects the actual last update to help search engines crawl efficiently.
  • Split Large Sitemaps: If your site has over 50,000 URLs, split into multiple sitemaps and use a sitemap index file.
  • Exclude Non-SEO Pages: Avoid including cart, checkout, or user account pages in your sitemap.

Top 5 Mistakes to Avoid in XML Sitemaps

Published on July 21, 2025

Creating an XML sitemap is simple, but small mistakes can harm your SEO. Here are five common pitfalls to avoid:

  • Including Non-Canonical URLs: Ensure all URLs in your sitemap are canonical to avoid duplicate content issues.
  • Incorrect Priority Values: Don’t set all pages to 1.0; reserve high priority (0.8-1.0) for key pages.
  • Outdated Lastmod Dates: Always update to reflect the latest changes.
  • Broken Links: Regularly check for 404 errors in your sitemap using tools like Screaming Frog.
  • Ignoring Sitemap Size Limits: Keep sitemaps under 50,000 URLs or 50MB, or use a sitemap index.
